The origins of christmas

Arely Ortega Chavez

Christmas origins blend the biblical story of Jesus's birth with ancient pagan winter solstice festivals like Saturnalia (Roman) and Yule (Norse), with the date December 25th strategically chosen by the early Church to co-opt these popular celebrations and replace them with Christian meaning, integrating traditions like feasting, gift-giving, evergreen decorations (mistletoe, holly), and feasting to symbolize the "rebirth of the sun" with the birth of Jesus, the "Light of the World," evolving over centuries into the holiday we know today.



Christmas started as a Christian holiday to celebrate Jesus' birth, formalized around 336 AD in Rome, possibly to overlay the existing pagan winter festivals like Saturnalia and the birth of the sun god Sol Invictus, absorbing some traditions while creating a Christian alternative. While the Bible doesn't state Jesus' birth date, early Christian thinkers linked December 25th through calculations, with evidence suggesting the date was set as early as the 2nd century by theologians like Hippolytus. Over centuries, cultural elements like Santa Claus and Christmas trees were integrated, solidifying it as a global holiday.
